# Magic Item Examples by Character Level
## Realistic Loadouts Within Wealth Budget

This document shows example magic item loadouts for different character types at various levels, staying within the wealth by level budget from the DMG.

---

## Level 3 Cleric (Wisdom-based Support)
**Total Wealth**: 2,700 gp  
**Mundane Equipment Cost**: ~600 gp (scale mail, heavy wooden shield, morningstar, holy symbol, etc.)  
**Magic Item Budget**: ~2,100 gp

### Magic Items
1. **Wand of Cure Light Wounds** (CL 1, 50 charges) - 750 gp (ESSENTIAL for healing)
2. **Cloak of Resistance +1** - 1,000 gp
3. **Scrolls** (Bless, Shield of Faith, Remove Fear) - 350 gp

**Total Magic Cost**: 2,100 gp  
**Remaining**: 0 gp

### Character Impact
- Healing: 50d8+50 available (average 275 HP healing for 750 gp!)
- Saves: +1 to all (Fort/Ref/Will)
- Combat: Keep mundane weapon/armor (you're still in melee)
- Utility: Scroll backup for situational spells

### Notes
**Wand of CLW is THE most cost-effective item in D&D**:
- 750 gp for 50 charges
- Average 5.5 HP per charge = 275 HP total
- **15 gp per heal** vs 10 gp for Potion of CLW (1d8+1 each)
- Every cleric, druid, and ranger should have one!

## Level 4 Sorcerer (Charisma-based Blaster)
**Total Wealth**: 5,600 gp  
**Mundane Equipment Cost**: ~200 gp (robes, light crossbow, component pouch, etc.)  
**Magic Item Budget**: ~5,400 gp

### Magic Items
1. **Cloak of Charisma +2** - 4,000 gp (PRIORITY - spell DC)
2. **Cloak of Resistance +1** - 1,000 gp
3. **Wand of Mage Armor** (CL 1) - 750 gp

**Total Magic Cost**: 5,750 gp  
**Over budget by**: 350 gp

### Adjusted Build
1. **Cloak of Charisma +2** - 4,000 gp
2. **Wand of Mage Armor** - 750 gp
3. **Wand of Shield** - 750 gp

**Total**: 5,500 gp  
**Remaining**: -100 gp (slightly over, skip mundane crossbow)

### Character Impact
- Spell DC: +1 (from Cha +2)
- Bonus Spells: +1 per spell level
- AC: +8 when buffed (Mage Armor +4, Shield +4)
- Saves: +1 Will (from Cha increase)
- Defense: 100 minutes of Mage Armor, 50 minutes of Shield

### Notes
**Level 4 is the EARLIEST you can afford a +2 stat boost (4,000 gp)**:
- Total wealth at L4 is 5,600 gp
- Stat boosts are THE priority (affects spell DC, bonus spells, saves)
- Defensive wands are next priority (no armor proficiency!)
- Skip Cloak of Resistance until Level 5 (not enough gold)

**Sorcerers vs Wizards**:
- Sorcerers want Charisma (Cloak slot)
- Wizards want Intelligence (Headband slot)
- Both need saves eventually (Cloak of Resistance conflicts with Charisma cloaks!)
- Solution: At higher levels, use Amulet of Natural Armor + Ring of Protection instead

## Key Takeaways

### Wealth Progression Reality
- **Levels 1-5**: Can afford 1-3 basic items (weapon, armor, cloak)
- **Levels 6-10**: Full Big Six starts coming together
- **Levels 11-15**: Big Six complete, start adding situational items
- **Levels 16-20**: Big Six maxed, add luxury items (flight, displacement, freedom)

### Priority Order Works
1. Weapon/Armor enhancements first
2. Primary stat booster second
3. Defensive items third (cloak, ring, amulet)
4. Secondary stats fourth
5. Utility/situational last

### Slot Management Matters
At high levels, characters run into **slot conflicts**:
- Shoulders: Cloak of Resistance vs Cloak of Charisma vs Wings of Flying vs Cloak of Displacement
- Neck: Amulet of Natural Armor vs Periapt of Wisdom vs Amulet of Health
- Rings: Only 2 slots, many good options

### Class-Specific Priorities
- **Fighters/Barbarians**: Weapon/Armor first, then Str/Con
- **Rogues/Rangers**: Dex items first (attacks and AC both benefit)
- **Clerics/Druids**: Wisdom (spell DC), then defensive items
- **Wizards/Sorcerers**: Mental stats (spells), then pure defense (no armor/weapons)
- **Paladins**: Str (damage), Cha (spells/abilities), defensive items
